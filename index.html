The Core Question

The pressure is building long before you see it

Right now, in your area, people are quietly searching for help.

Not with forms.
Not with filters.
With real questions.

“Why do I feel so anxious.”
“I cannot pay the rent this month.”
“Mum is getting worse. I do not know what to do.”

Most of this never reaches your dashboards.
You only see the late stage: crisis, appointments, unplanned care.

What if you could see the early signals, in plain English, and act before things broke.

A human moment

Meet Sarah. Thirty four. Awake at 11pm.

She is not filling in a form.
She is not choosing from a menu of services.

She is just saying what is going on.

“I feel anxious all the time. I am not sleeping. I do not want to bother my GP.”

The system understands enough.
Not everything. Just enough.

A Support Playlist appears. A small, human set of next steps:

  • A local walking group
  • Your mental health service, with a simple self referral
  • A trusted online programme for anxiety
  • A short explainer on sleep, written in plain English

She does not need to know the name of a service.
She does not need to understand the system.

She just says what is happening.
The Support Playlist quietly gathers what might help.

Plain English.
Human shaped.
Not diagnosis.
Not treatment.
A smarter way to navigate support.

The learning layer

From one story to genuine intelligence

Every Support Playlist begins as a human moment.
But together, they form a pattern.

This is Demand Shaping Intelligence.

It focuses on signals, not identities:

  • How many people are asking for help with anxiety
  • Where money, food, and housing appear together
  • When carers are looking for support
  • What people try before approaching the GP
  • Which needs rise first, and where

DSI turns everyday requests into a live view of demand.

It helps you:

  • Spot pressure building early
  • Strengthen non clinical options
  • Shape provision rather than react to crisis

This is not another reporting tool.
It is a learning layer that becomes more useful every day.

People found it directly.
People found it through Google.
People arrived from Microsoft Teams, pcnquicklinks.org and thelocalsocial.org.

The ecosystem is beginning to work as intended.

The strongest signal is mental health and wellbeing:

  • 55 unique visitors
  • 71 total visits

Followed by healthy living, then crisis and safety.

People are also looking for:

  • Caring for someone
  • Food and money help
  • Community support
  • Family and parenting
  • Home and housing

Framing the site around everyday needs, not clinical labels, is working.
People see themselves immediately.

Most use Microsoft Edge, Chrome or Safari, suggesting both NHS staff and residents are moving through the same front door.

In one month, the pilot is already:

  • Revealing emotional strain
  • Showing prevention in action
  • Validating a community based model
  • Demonstrating a real appetite for non clinical support

The place based view

Seeing your neighbourhood in real time

When Support Playlists and DSI combine, something more powerful emerges.

Neighbourhood Intelligence.

This is not surveillance.
It is not tracking individuals.

It is the shape of need across a place.

For PCNs, ICSs, Integrated Neighbourhood Teams and local partners, it shows:

  • Where emotional pressure is rising
  • Which communities need food or money support
  • How often carers are searching for help
  • Whether people are trying healthy living options before crisis
  • The impact of new services as they go live

It is anonymous.
It is aggregated.
It is local.

It gives you a shared map of strain, resilience, and opportunity.
A clearer way to target effort where it matters most.
